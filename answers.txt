sort1 uses: Bubble Sort

How do you know?: It is much faster when the input is already sorted, wich is a typical behavior of Bubble Sort.

sort2 uses: Merge Sort

How do you know?: The running time is almost the same for random, sorted, and reversed inputs, wich is characteristics of Merge Sort (0(n log n)).

sort3 uses: Selection Sort

How do you know?: The running time is similar regardless of the initial order of the numbers, which is typical of Selection SOrt (0(n²)).
